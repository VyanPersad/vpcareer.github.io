Laptop Cooling Pad

×

Final Design Render

Description

The aim of the project was to design build and test a device that can lower the temperature of a laptop. In addition it aimed to investigate the effect of the lowered temperature on the performance of the laptop.

The laptop pad was designed, built and tested. The testing consisted of both virtual and real world testing in addition to verification by manual calculation. The virtual component utilized SolidWorks COSMOS and FloXpress to perform air flow, thermal, stress and displacement finite element analyses on the design.

The real world testing consisted of measuring the CPU performance, battery life, laptop temperatures, PCMark test results and the measurement of program load times. The project built pad was also compared to a commercially available generic store bought cooling pad.

It was found from the virtual testing that the design put forward was in fact worthy of building. It proved that the design elements (eg. Aluminum heat sink) performed as they would be expected to. The real world testing proved that the cooling pad improves laptop performance and response time, reduces laptop operating temperatures but at the same time reduces battery life.

The laptop cooling pad was built, tested and found to increase the performance of the laptop in all the suggested areas. In comparison it outperforms the store bought cooling pad in all the measured engineering oriented characteristics, however it is second in the rank to the store bought when one considers the consumer oriented characteristics.

PetroCalc

×

Description

The app was designed to be a tool for both student and professional in the oil and gas sector, targeting both by delivering content specifically designed to be used by both parties. It was designed to be an electronic learning device to students and go on to be a calculator for oil and gas professionals. It aims to provide a cheaper platform for oil and gas software, one that is also more mobile and easier to use.

It was coded in android, the programming language, for mobile handsets using the android OS. It was developed using the Android Studios IDE and uploaded to the web using the Google Play developer website. The software IDE allowed for the app to be tested for stability on various handsets and aided in the troubleshooting of problems in the design process. The second part of the project was the hosting of material online, it allowed for distributing material via another platform and providing content and features that are not otherwise available on the app.

The app gives the student access to content that is tailored from the petroleum engineering course at the University of the West Indies, a course layout that is applicable to students locally and internationally. The professional has access to a series of mathematical calculators that aid in executing common engineering calculations pertaining to the field.

Once uploaded the app’s downloads exceeded 500+ downloads, with a retention rate of 60% as of the writing of this report, indicating a demand for the app and the service that it provides. The number of downloads internationally has exceeded local downloads, proving the concept and the content are applicable to the international oil and gas industry. Geographically the app’s downloads directly correlate to other major international oil and gas producers. This popularity points to the applicability of the app to the industry and the intended markets.
