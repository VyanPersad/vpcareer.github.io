The Abstract

The aim of the project was to design build and test a device that can lower the temperature of a laptop. In addition it aimed to investigate the effect of the lowered temperature on the performance of the laptop.

The laptop pad was designed, built and tested. The testing consisted of both virtual and real world testing in addition to verification by manual calculation. The virtual component utilized SolidWorks COSMOS and FloXpress to perform air flow, thermal, stress and displacement finite element analyses on the design.

The real world testing consisted of measuring the CPU performance, battery life, laptop temperatures, PCMark test results and the measurement of program load times. The project built pad was also compared to a commercially available generic store bought cooling pad.

It was found from the virtual testing that the design put forward was in fact worthy of building. It proved that the design elements (eg. Aluminum heat sink) performed as they would be expected to. The real world testing proved that the cooling pad improves laptop performance and response time, reduces laptop operating temperatures but at the same time reduces battery life.

The laptop cooling pad was built, tested and found to increase the performance of the laptop in all the suggested areas. In comparison it outperforms the store bought cooling pad in all the measured engineering oriented characteristics, however it is second in the rank to the store bought when one considers the consumer oriented characteristics.

The Results

The aim of the project was to design build and test a device that can lower the temperature of a laptop. In addition it aimed to investigate the effect of the lowered temperature on the performance of the laptop. This was accomplished by first designing and iterating through 5 possible designs. The designs were appraised and the desired criteria determined by research and comparisons to xisting products.

Once the designs were approved they were ranked and the best choosen, or a new design based on the favourable features of all was decided upon. This design was then modelled in SolidWorks and various simulations run to simulate the real world. Mathematical calculations were also done to confirm the accuracy of the model, of which they closely matched the modelled results.

The final part of the project was to build and test it. It was built and tested and the performance was appraised against a store bought model and the performance of a laptop without any cooling pad. It was found to lower the temperature and thus improve performance. It's performance renders the project a success.
