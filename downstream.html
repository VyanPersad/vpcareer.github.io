Refinery at Sunrise

Summary

This section is a brief look at what was done within the downstream plant based section of the oil and gas industry. I was a part of this sector as a graduate engineer stationed primarily within the design department at the Pinte a Pierre refinery.

While here I saw the multitude of individual plants that worked to the make the refinery a single cohesive unit. Stationed within the design department I got to see how the process of troubleshooting and solution generating goes about within the plant environment.

The ability to see and be part of the design process of solutions and to see some of it put into action was an accomplishment for me. It is always an eye opening experience to see the other wise abstract mathematics taught in school be put to use practically to generate and prove a solution that works physically in the real world.

Quick Glance

  • Link-seal sizing
  • General inspections and appraisal of problems
  • Pipe work design and support infrastructure
  • Walk-arounds and basic introductions to the various plants

Work Experience

The department is responsible for the specification of equipment to be use on the refinery floor. In most cases the request for the specification comes from the process department, they will determine the process and produce the required documents and calculations to guide the design department. The design department will then investigate the site and determine the best way forward to execute the job.

During the design process of the job the design engineer will call upon the services of the drawing department. This department is staffed with draughts persons who assist with the production of the required drawings that illustrate the existing or the potential situation on site. During the job the engineer will consult with these draughts persons drawing upon their knowledge and experience base to generate further possible solutions to the problem/task at hand.

After the design of the job it has to be written up this is where a BM is proposed. The BM as it called is actually a Bill of Materials, which by its name is misleading. It is actually the documentation of the sequence of events that must take place for the job to be executed properly. It also details the equipment and the materials to be used to fulfill the job requirements. The BM will specify the appropriate standards that are to be used. It is a Scope of Works for the job yet to be done.

On-Site

Whilst at the refinery I was assigned to an engineer during which time I assisted with some calculations regarding the Constant Effort Spring Supports on 4 VDU, having my name added to the BM as a result. As this situation unfolded I kept in contact with the engineer assisting and volunteering where possible, also accompanying out to the plant floor to aid in the investigation of the plant conditions.

In other situations my exposure covered a greater area of the refinery compound extending from North Area: The Machine, Tube and Bundle Shops, 4 VDU, 2 Girbotol, the API Oil-Water Separators and F.C.C.U. onwards to the West Area: The Simpsons Sumps, The tank farm and The Berths.
