Summary

This section is a brief look at what was done within the upstream drilling and workpover sector of the oil and gas industry. It highlights what was done experienced and learnt during my time within this sector.

My introduction to the field was as a Graduate Mechanical Engineer in the Petrotrin Horizon's Graduate Trainee Programme. This began in Santa Flora where I was posted to the land based drilling operations of Petrotrin. I would subsequently go on to be witness to offshore drilling operations as well.

Overall the experience constituted one of the more rarified uses of a Mechanical Engineer as a drilling engineer.

Work Experience

Assissted with the implementation of an asset improvement and management system which was to be used on all the tools in the service of the drilling and workover department utilised by the comapny's land based drilling and workover department.This project saw the design of a maintenance schedule for the tools in service. It involved looking over said tools and determining what is the best way to inspect and service it in house or if third party services would be needed.

A register of all the tools in service was compiled and the appropriate industrial standard was found and applied to the equipment. This meant working with API or specialty standards that detail the tools in use. If none were present or if it was found the maintenance schedule to be lacking then manufacturer's documentation was consulted

Rig 1

Was tasked with assisting with the preperation of a tender for maintenance services. I was given the resposibility of drafting the technical specifications needed to be executed by the intended service provider. This involved looking at current usage and determining the appropriate duty cycles in addition to looking over prescribed industry or manufacturer standards for maintenance.

On-Site

During my respective jobs time was spent on the drilling floor, this was a unique and valuable experience as it allowed me to see the tangible results of planned operations. The hands-on work exposed me to the dynamism of the workk floor and allowed me to physically work alongside industry professionals. I was there to provide engineering support for the operations at hand, offering solutions and troubleshooting as needed. This meant working with both the on-site crew and service providers to go through available options and execute the desired options.

On a daily basis I was responsible for the daily costing of operations based on pre-determined metrics. Operations were appraised based on the plan and deviations were evaluated and accounted for. Any deviation in operations from the plan had to be justified technically and accounted for financially.
