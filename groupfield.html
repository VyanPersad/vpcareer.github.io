Group Field

This is a group project that is given to MSc final year students working as a group. This project allows students to plan and manage a theoretical oil and gas field based on real world data.

In this case the field used was an offshore oil field set in the general area that exists off shore the south western peninsula of Trinidad and Tobago.

I was responsible for the crafting of the drilling programme, the design of the top side production facilities and the HSE policy that governs the operations in the field.

Drilling

  • Tasks executed to make the drilling section:
  • Made the pore pressure profile using dExponent values from drilling reports
  • Determined the Mud weight profile
  • Determined the drilling fluid/mud type for each section
  • Plotted the hole sections and the casing profiles for each
  • Determined the cementing volumes and blends/composition
  • Selected the appropriate drilling assemblies to minimise drilling time
  • Ploted the drill time curve for the xpected timeline for operations

Production Facilities

Worked with other team members to design the topside production facilities. The design hinged based on expected production output for the wells to be drilled. It was done by consulting existing offshore drilling structure standards for the platfrom itself and using process and API standards for sizing the facility equipment. Typical equipment used in the design were test, 3-phase separators and a surge seperator.

HSE

This section focused on the health, safety and environmental policies that the proposed company would use when operating in the field.

Overall a vey simple derivative of a Plan Do Check Act system was implemented based largely arounf ISO and OSHA policies. Both of which were very common in industry.

The policy governs several aspects of oil and gas activities in the proposed field. It covers hazardous operations and environmental threats that would be faced while operating in the area. Also identified are possible mitigations strategies and worst case scenarios for incidents and accidents.
